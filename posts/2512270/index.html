This article introduces the specific method of implementing the MacOS liquid glass effect in the Hexo Butterfly theme. It discusses in detail the implementation approach based on multi-layer stacking (including container shell, liquid blur layer, tint mask, highlight layer, and content layer) combined with SVG filters, and summarizes the hover interactive dynamic effects and the modification steps for practical application in the theme.

Saw it on Codepen, made some slight modifications. You can see the effect on the aside card or nav of this site.

Implementation Approach

The main idea is to use multi-layer stacking, with a total of four layers

liquidGlass-wrapper

Container Shell

  • Function: The outermost container, responsible for overall shadow and animation transitions.
  • Key Points：
    • background: noneandbackdrop-filter: noneis to prevent it from generating blur itself, delegating the blur to the internal.liquidGlass-effect。
    • Shadows simulate the three-dimensional feel of glass.
    • transitionMake the changes in border-radius and padding during hover smoother.

liquidGlass-effect

Core “Liquid” Blur Layer

  • backdrop-filter: blur(3px)
    Blur the background content (e.g., other parts of the webpage) → This is the foundation of “frosted glass”.
  • filter: url(#glass-distortion) ⭐ This is the key to the “liquid” feel! It references an SVG filter (#glass-distortion), usually defined in the page’s<svg>to applydistortion, ripples, liquid flowand other effects.
  • isolation: isolate Ensure this layer creates a new stacking context to prevent the filter from affecting other elements.

glass-distortion

liquidGlass-tint

Tint Overlay

  • Overlay a layer ofsemi-transparent white(can also be changed to light blue, light gray, etc.).
  • Simulate the “reflective base color” of real glass, making text/content more readable.
  • z-index: 1Ensure it is above the effect and below the shine.

liquidGlass-shine

Highlight layer

  • Useinset box-shadowto simulate the glass edge’shighlights and subtle reflections。
  • Top-right bright (+2px white light), bottom-left slightly dark (-1px white light) → creates a sense of light direction.
  • z-index: 2Place it on the topmost layer (but below the content).

liquidGlass-box

Content layer

  • The actual place for content (e.g., avatar, text).
  • z-index: 3Ensure content is above all glass effect layers and clearly visible.
  • position: relativeTake it out of the normal flow, but still within the wrapper.

Interactive effects (Hover dynamics)

  • On hover：
    • Border radius increases (+1em) → simulates ‘liquid expansion’ or ‘soft deformation’;
    • Increased padding → overall ‘breathing space’;
    • All non-content layers synchronize corner radius changes to maintain visual consistency.
