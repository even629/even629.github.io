Hexo butterfly theme implementing MacOS liquid glass effect

This article introduces the specific method for implementing the MacOS liquid glass effect in the Hexo Butterfly theme. The article discusses in detail the implementation approach based on multi-layer overlays (including container shell, liquid blur layer, tint mask layer, highlight layer, and content layer) and SVG filters, and summarizes the Hover interaction dynamic effects and the modification steps for practical application in the theme.

Saw it on Codepen, modified it slightly. The effect can be seen in the aside card or nav of this site.

Implementation approach

The main idea is to use multi-layer overlays, totaling four layers

liquidGlass-wrapper

Container shell

  • Function: The outermost container, responsible for the overall shadow and animation transitions.
  • Key point：
    • background: noneandbackdrop-filter: noneis to prevent it from blurring itself, leaving the blur to the interior.liquidGlass-effect。
    • The shadow simulates the three-dimensional feel of glass.
    • transitionMake the border-radius and padding changes smoother on hover.

liquidGlass-effect

Core “liquid” blur layer

  • backdrop-filter: blur(3px)
    Blur the background content (like the rest of the webpage) → This is the basis of “frosted glass”.
  • filter: url(#glass-distortion) ⭐ This is the key to the “liquid” feel! It references an SVG filter (#glass-distortion), usually defined in the page’s<svg>, used to apply to the imagedistortion, ripple, liquid flowand other effects.
  • isolation: isolate Ensure this layer creates a new stacking context to prevent the filter from affecting other elements.

glass-distortion

liquidGlass-tint

Tint overlay

  • Overlay a layer on top of the blur layersemi-transparent white(can also be changed to light blue, light gray, etc.).
  • Simulates the “reflective base” of real glass, making text/content easier to read.
  • z-index: 1Ensure it is above the effect and below the shine.

liquidGlass-shine

Highlight layer

  • Use Inner shadow (inset box-shadow) Simulate the glass edge’shighlights and micro-reflections。
  • Top right bright (+2px white light), bottom left slightly dark (-1px white light) → creating a sense of light direction.
  • z-index: 2Placed on the topmost layer (but below the content).

liquidGlass-box

Content layer

  • Where the actual content is placed (e.g., avatars, text).
  • z-index: 3Ensure the content is above all glass effect layers, clearly visible.
  • position: relativeTake it out of the normal flow, but keep it inside the wrapper.

Interaction effects (Hover dynamics)

  • On hover：
    • Border radius increases (+1em) → simulating “liquid expansion” or “soft deformation”;
    • Increase padding → overall ‘breathing room’;
    • Synchronize border-radius changes across all non-content layers to maintain visual consistency.
